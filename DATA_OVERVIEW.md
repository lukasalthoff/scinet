# SciNet Data Overview

SciNet maps the anatomy of scientific research across 30 fields, 300+ subfields, and 4,516 topics. This page provides a visual summary of the database — task structure, research scale, verifiability, and AI adoption across disciplines.

Figures are generated by [`generate_figures.py`](generate_figures.py) and can be refreshed by running `python3 generate_figures.py` from the `public/` directory.

## Verifiability

SciNet computes a verifiability index for each subfield based on three components: retraction rate, hedging language frequency, and booster language frequency in paper abstracts. Higher scores indicate more cautious, verifiable research communication.

**Verifiability by domain** — distribution of subfield-level verifiability scores across the four research domains.

<p align="center"><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/lukasalthoff/scinet/main/figures/verifiability_by_domain.png" alt="Verifiability by domain" width="680"/></p>

**Top and bottom subfields** — the 10 subfields with the highest and lowest verifiability index (among subfields with ≥ 100 papers).

<p align="center"><img src="https://raw.githubusercontent.com/lukasalthoff/scinet/main/figures/verifiability_top_bottom.png" alt="Top and bottom subfields by verifiability" width="900"/></p>

**Verifiability components by domain** — paper-weighted averages of retraction rate, hedging language, and booster language across domains, showing how each component drives the overall index.

## Claude Usage and Research Output

**Claude usage vs. research output per capita** — scatter plot comparing country-level Claude usage intensity against total scientific papers produced per 1,000 working-age residents ([OpenAlex](https://openalex.org/)). Each bubble represents one country, sized by total publication volume. Countries above the trend line produce more research per capita than their Claude usage would predict — and vice versa.

Claude usage data: [Anthropic Economic Index](https://www.anthropic.com/research/anthropic-economic-index-september-2025-report), August 2025 ([dataset on Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/datasets/Anthropic/EconomicIndex)).

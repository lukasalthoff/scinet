# Survey Questions

This document reflects the live survey definitions in [`docs/contribute.js`](../docs/contribute.js).
It lists the current answerable questions in each module and excludes explanatory `info` blocks.

---

## Your Research (18)

1. About how many hours per week do you personally spend on each activity category?
2. Which AI tools do you currently use for research?
3. How frequently do you use AI tools in your research?
4. About how many hours per week do you actively interact with AI as part of your research?
5. How much do you spend on AI tools per month (subscriptions, API access, etc.)?
6. For which research tasks do you find AI most useful?
7. For **your own research**, what percentage of your total research time does AI currently save you?
8. For the **median researcher in your field**, what percentage of total research time do you think AI currently saves?
9. For **your own research**, where do you currently see yourself on the AI usage in science spectrum?
10. For the **median researcher in your field**, where do you think they currently sit on the AI usage in science spectrum?
11. How well-informed do you feel about AI capabilities relevant to your research?
12. How confident are you in your ability to detect AI-generated errors in your own work?
13. How easy is it for you to verify or replicate research findings in your peers' work?
14. How significantly do you expect AI to change your research over the next 5 years?
15. Overall, do you expect AI’s impact on your research to be positive or negative?
16. How has AI changed the type of research questions you pursue?
17. For each activity category, roughly what percentage of time has AI saved you?
18. How has AI changed the skill level needed to perform tasks in each category?

Note: the activity-category questions now use 8 categories: Reading & Knowledge Acquisition, Ideation & Hypothesis Generation, Data Gathering, Data Analysis, Writing & Communication, Peer Review & Service, Mentorship & Teaching, and Administration.

## AI in Your Research (9)

1. How do you tend to learn about new AI tools and how to use them?
2. For which research tasks is AI **less** useful or counterproductive?
3. Which skills do you expect to become more valuable for you in the AI era?
4. Which skills do you expect to become less important or obsolete?
5. What specific changes in research practices do you anticipate? Select all that apply. If you select "Other," describe any additional change in research practice you anticipate.
6. In your experience as a peer reviewer, how has the volume of submissions changed over the past two years?
7. In your experience as a peer reviewer, how has the average quality of submissions changed over the past two years?
8. Anything else you have noticed as a reviewer?
9. What are your main concerns about AI in research? Select all that apply. If you selected "Other," describe any additional concern.

## AI Impact on Your Research (4)

1. For an experienced researcher in your subfield, what percentage of time savings does AI currently provide for each activity?
2. Now consider a junior researcher (less than 3 years post-PhD). What percentage of time savings does AI currently provide for each activity?
3. Which activities could CURRENT AI perform entirely without human oversight, at acceptable quality in your subfield?
4. Which activities do you expect AI could fully automate BY 2030?

## Verifiable Predictions (14)

1. What share of papers in your field will have a first or corresponding author with no publication in the field in the prior 5 years?
2. What will be the median years from an author's first publication in any field to their first publication in your field?
3. What share of papers in your field will come from institutions outside the top decile of field R&D spenders?
4. What share of research-related job postings in your field will require 3 or more years of field-specific experience?
5. Do you expect the complexity of skill requirements in research job postings in your field to increase or decrease? Give a value relative to today (`100 = no change`, below `100 = simpler`, above `100 = more complex`).
6. What share of research-related job postings in your field will require a PhD?
7. What will be the median annual publication count per R1-university faculty member in your field?
8. What share of active publishing researchers in your field will publish 5 or more papers in a single year?
9. What will be the median months from preprint posting to journal publication in your field?
10. What share of publications in your field will explicitly use AI/ML methods (based on title or abstract keywords)?
11. What percent change in PhD enrollment do you expect in your field (relative to 2024)?
12. What will be the median years from doctoral program start to research doctorate completion in your field?
13. What share of new doctorate recipients in your field (excluding postdocs) will take jobs in business/industry?
14. What share of papers in your field will include at least one private-sector company-affiliated coauthor?

## Predict the Median (4)

1. For the **median researcher in your field**, how much has AI reduced the skill level needed to do publishable-quality empirical research?
2. For the **median researcher in your field**, by 2030, what percentage of research tasks will be substantially augmented by AI?
3. For **your own answer**, by 2030, how many years of post-PhD training will be needed before a researcher can independently publish in your subfield?
4. For the **median researcher in your field**, by 2030, how many years of post-PhD training do you think will be needed before a researcher can independently publish in your subfield?

## Measuring Simplification: A Toolkit (21)

1. Using the tools researchers currently have access to (including AI), what is the minimum career stage to perform each task to a publishable standard?
2. If AI and LLM tools were NOT available, what would be the minimum career stage?
3. Estimate years of specialized training required for each task: current (with AI) vs. if AI unavailable.
4. Could you now delegate a literature review to a less experienced researcher who has AI tools, and expect similar quality output?
5. Could you now delegate a statistical analysis to a less experienced researcher with AI?
6. Given one week to produce a systematic literature review, who produces higher quality output?
7. Given 40 hours to complete an empirical analysis, who produces higher quality output?
8. Without AI tools, what is the least experienced person who could produce a publishable-quality literature review in your subfield?
9. With AI tools, what is the least experienced person who could produce a publishable-quality literature review?
10. Think about the most recent paper or project you worked on. For each skill below, how much did AI tools reduce the effort you personally needed?
11. If a 2nd-year PhD (with AI) and a senior professor (without AI) each wrote a literature review, what’s the probability a blind evaluator could NOT tell which was which?
12. Same question for a statistical / empirical analysis.
13. Same question for writing a first draft of a paper.
14. Same question for designing a study / experiment.
15. In the past 12 months, which tasks have you assigned to a less experienced researcher than you would have chosen before AI tools?
16. Compared to 3 years ago, how have the technical skill requirements for a research assistant position in your lab changed?
17. In 2020 (pre-AI), what was the minimum career stage to do this independently?
18. In 2025 (with current AI tools), what is the minimum career stage?
19. By 2030 (your prediction), what will be the minimum career stage?
20. Literature Review: Time by Experience Level.
21. Literature Review: Quality by Experience Level.

## Measuring Augmentation: A Toolkit (22)

1. Literature review: hours without AI vs. with AI.
2. Empirical analysis: hours without AI vs. with AI.
3. Writing research code: hours without AI vs. with AI.
4. First draft of a paper: hours without AI vs. with AI.
5. For writing a research paper, what % of time does AI save in each phase?
6. For a data analysis project, what % of time does AI save in each phase?
7. How many papers / manuscripts did you produce per year BEFORE using AI tools (e.g., in 2022)?
8. How many papers / manuscripts are you producing per year NOW (2025–2026)?
9. Of any increase in your output, what percentage would you attribute specifically to AI tools?
10. When AI saves you time on a research task, how do you typically use the savings?
11. Since you began using AI tools, how has the quality of your research output changed?
12. Has AI enabled you to attempt more ambitious research projects than you otherwise would have?
13. For a ROUTINE literature review, what % of time does AI save?
14. For a NOVEL literature review (unfamiliar topic), what % of time does AI save?
15. For ROUTINE coding (standard methods), what % of time does AI save?
16. For NOVEL coding (new algorithm, complex optimization), what % of time does AI save?
17. In general, does AI save you more time on routine or novel tasks?
18. By end of 2027, what percentage of total research time in your field will AI save for a typical researcher?
19. By end of 2030?
20. What is the MAXIMUM percentage of research time that AI could EVER save in your field, even with arbitrarily advanced AI?
21. Empirical Analysis Task.
22. In general, does AI save a larger PERCENTAGE of time for junior or senior researchers in your field?
